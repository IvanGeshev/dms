## Company context

You are designing the homepage for Designed Maintenance Services Inc.
(DMS), a turn-key industrial contracting company based in Atlanta,
Georgia. DMS provides mechanical, electrical, and process piping
services to high-stakes, regulated facilities, backed by a guaranteed
1.5-hour average emergency response across Georgia. The page must
instantly communicate professionalism, speed, and credibility, and drive
two actions: requesting a quote and calling the emergency line.

## Brand

- Use the attached logo exactly. Do not alter, recolor, or restyle it.

- Theme: this is a DARK-theme website. Build from four color roles — a
  dark base (backgrounds and surfaces), light (primary text and
  high-contrast surfaces), and two warm accents, yellow and orange,
  reserved for accents and CTAs. Use the accents sparingly and
  purposefully; most of the page is dark and light.

- Suggested accent split (adjust if a better system emerges): orange as
  the dominant CTA color (“Request a Quote” buttons), yellow for urgency
  and the 24-hour emergency topbar. Don't let the two accents compete —
  one leads, one supports.

- Execute the dark theme against the attached DMS Design & Build
  Standards: verify contrast (light text must clear 4.5:1 on the dark
  base; large text 3:1), set color-scheme: dark on <html> and match
  <meta name="theme-color"> to the background, and follow the
  anti-slop rules. This is a deliberate dark choice for an industrial
  brand — hazard-yellow and safety-orange on a dark, heavy-equipment
  base — not dark-by-default.

- Everything else — typography, spacing, components, layout — is open
  within that dark, industrial, high-trust direction. Serious and
  professional, not flashy or playful.

## Rules (follow exactly)

1.  Build the sections in the exact order listed below. Do not add,
    remove, merge, or reorder sections.

2.  The header is persistent/sticky and stays visible while the user
    scrolls.

3.  Place the 24-hour emergency phone line in a thin topbar ABOVE the
    main navbar.

4.  Design desktop-first, but make the page fully responsive for tablet
    and mobile.

5.  Keep a strong visual hierarchy, accessible contrast (WCAG AA), and
    one obvious primary action per section.

6.  Use video placeholders wherever video is specified, and clean
    iconography for value props and industries.

7.  Output one cohesive, polished homepage. I may ask for alternate
    visual directions afterward.
